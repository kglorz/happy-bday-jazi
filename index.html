Happy Birthday, Jazi

to you whom I love most

"To love is to burn a little less than what risks extinguishing both flames all together." 'Tis but an anecdote that came to mind after watching "The Loved Ones" for the first time. At that moment, I believed I had witnessed a glimpse of the bitter truths that linger around love and relationships, but albeit being grounded in reality, it was simply too accommodating to the narrative depicted on the silver screen. In the real world, the volatile nature of emotions, akin to fire, paints romance as sometimes a tragic conflagration, and other times a gentle, soothing ember at the fireplace. And at the cusp of this reverie was you. The fire that often cradles, yet also burns, but more importantly, the fire I'd ruthlessly keep aflame even through the harshest winters. Meeting you was totally unexpected, an organic encounter in the most genuine sense. I entered our purcom classroom expecting nothing more than another mundane GEC class session, yet I was met with a visage of beauty that was truly mesmerizing. It wasn't at that moment that I realized I've loved again, but it did, however, make me more eager to attend our classes every Tuesday and Friday. So much so that I had to use the bingo as an excuse to talk to you again despite our prior interaction. Since then, you've been immortalized as "purcom baddie" in our friend group and I have myself to blame for that. Ever since then, every meeting gradually mustered up in me the courage to add you on Facebook, and through whatever stroke of luck, you chose to follow me on Instagram, to which I am truly grateful for. We started chatting and, to be honest, I didn't know what to expect. What I was sure of was that I had come to like you across the days that we've endlessly talked 'til midnight. Seeing your notifications and you're active response to my messages was truly bliss. Although you diverged a lot from my initial expectations, it did nothing to lessen the growing love I had for you. It had me pinning on the hopes of a slow burn romance potentially brewing. You made me happy with a simple chat, a simple reel, and now a simple smile. When you pitched that spontaneous plan to drink coffee outside (what I consider as our first date of sorts), I was honestly a bit hesitant not because I wasn't interested, but because I was too inexperienced and I was afraid that I might fumble. Add to it the fact that you are really pretty, so I keep getting distracted while talking. When we spent all night laughing at reels until it was already 7 am, I smiled more than I did in the past couple of months prior. You brought genuine happiness that I've sought for such a long time from something as simple as talking and laughing together, and that's probably when I imagined spending the rest of my life with you. When we had our valentines escapade (which I consider another date), it was my first time spending time with someone I viewed romantically in the day of hearts. It was my first unboxing of collectibles, first arcade, first roaming around the mall, all with someone I love. Again, my heart became more certain. When we went on out first movie date, we watched the same movie that had given me a lot of realizations just days prior. For the second time, I watched "The Loved Ones," but now with a person I admire. When you leaned on my shoulder, butterflies spawned out of nowhere, a stark contrast to the tragedy of the movie we were watching. Back then, when I first watched that movie, I was disheartened by the sorrowful reality of pain and heartbreak because it was a painful reminder of the profound love that I felt I had wasted in the past, but the second time around, your warmth gave me hope. It gave me solace. Your embrace made me feel that maybe cupid had finally fired his evasive arrow through my aching heart. I went home that day with the widest smile I could ever muster in my entire life. I walked home wearing the smile you gave me. However, we both know that it wasn't all rainbows and sunshine, was it? When you first started coming over to my bh, our progress skyrocketed so fast that it became quite scary. All semblance of a slow burn romance was thrown out the window. We hit our highest highs so far and also our lowest lows. Together, we experienced a lot of firsts, but even still, we had so little time to build a proper, stable foundation for our relationship that, as the days went on, it felt like we were both treading unfamiliar waters. We were force to confront the side of each other that we haven't grown accustomed to yet. It became apparent in our frequent misunderstandings. Akin to fire, we burned too bright too fast that it genuinely felt like it was about to be extinguished. However, as I've said before, I didn't fall in love with you based on a mere spark. I love you in a way that even water can't ever hope to put out. This fire in my heart burns with a decisive passion fueled by choice. And I choose you every single day. The moment I wake up, I choose you. When I go to sleep, I choose you. When ever the clock ticks for every second, I choose you. Even when you're not on my mind, my heart chooses you. That's because I love you. There are still a lot of things I want to say, but I have so little time to write this letter. I've spent days trying to code this website for you and the last thing I did was write this letter. When we called yesterday, I was focused on finishing the website which is why I didn't respond that much. I am truly sorry. Even now that our purcom class is finished, I'm still here at the MIDSA office finishing the letter. So, I won't make this too long anymore. Again, happy birthday, Jazi! There's still a lot of things we have to figure out, and my wish is that we can continue to navigate this world together. Thank you for your existence, than you for being part of my life, and thank you for choosing me as well. I'll always be here and my love will remain unwavering even through the test of time. I loved you then, I love you now, and I will love you even after time ceases to exist.

Forever yours, Kenji
